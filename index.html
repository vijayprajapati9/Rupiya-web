What is a carbon credit?

A verified unit representing stored $\text{CO}_2$. Regenerative practices increase **Soil Organic Carbon (SOC)** which is verified using $\text{AI}$ and converted into marketable credits.

How it earns ‚Çπ800

Each verified credit pays the farmer approximately **‚Çπ800**. Verification is conducted through independent, certified partners (e.g., Carboneg) using digital traceability for transparency.

Quick Answers (FAQ)

‚è≥ Key Timeline:

Expect the first verifiable credits **12‚Äì24 months** after adopting regenerative practices (we handle the monitoring!).

**What is the cost or risk for the farmer?**

**Zero financial risk.** Rupiya.app covers all upfront costs for measurement, certification, and verification. We only take a share of the proceeds *after* the credits are successfully sold.

How is this different from regular fertilizer/inputs?

Fertilizer pays for crop yield. Carbon credits pay you for **soil health**‚Äîa second income stream. We specifically measure *new* carbon captured, rewarding you for climate-friendly changes you make.

How does Rupiya.app measure my soil without taking samples every year?

We use a **Hybrid Model**. We combine physical baseline samples with continuous **Satellite Data & AI Modeling** to track changes at scale. This keeps costs low for you while maintaining the high accuracy required by global verifiers.

Who buys the credits?

Carboneg and institutional buyers on the European & global voluntary carbon market.

Do farmers need to pay for certification?

No ‚Äî Rupiya.app covers verification costs and shares proceeds per the agreed model.
